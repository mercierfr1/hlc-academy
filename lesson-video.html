Mechanical Internal Structure + ChoCH

In this lesson, you'll learn about mechanical internal structure and how to identify Change of Character (ChoCH) patterns in the market. This is a crucial concept for understanding institutional order flow and market dynamics.

üìù Key Concepts

  • Internal Structure: The smaller time frame structure within larger time frame moves
  • ChoCH (Change of Character): When market structure shifts from bullish to bearish or vice versa
  • Order Flow: How institutional money flows into and out of the market
  • Structure Breaks: Key levels where market structure changes

üìù Lesson Notes

Mechanical Internal Structure:

  • Internal structure refers to the smaller time frame movements within larger time frame trends
  • It helps identify entry and exit points with higher precision
  • Key levels include swing highs, swing lows, and structure breaks

Change of Character (ChoCH):

  • ChoCH occurs when market structure shifts from one direction to another
  • It's a powerful signal that institutional money is changing direction
  • Look for breaks of previous swing highs/lows with momentum

Practical Application:

  • Use multiple time frames to confirm structure changes
  • Wait for confirmation before entering trades
  • Combine with supply and demand zones for optimal entries
